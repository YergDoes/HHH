Economic factors, such as poverty, unemployment and social inequalities, cause cities which are deemed to be unsafe due to crime caused by economic factors.
  • Based on the report and analyses conducted by the U.N., there will be an increase in people living in cities and urban areas, totaling 70% of the global population by 2050. These findings suggest that our infrastructure, sanitation, and the environment will be under tremendous pressure. Approximately 45% of the Philippine population is residing in urban areas, while poverty among the urban population is increasing due to the lack of adequate planning and the uneven distribution of resources. These problems point to the necessity of sustainable cities and viable communities as the next agenda.
